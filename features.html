Designing the Future: A Fusion of Interior Design, and Sustainable Architecture

Pushpender Ravish is a visionary architect dedicated to the principles of green building and sustainable architecture. With a passion for creating spaces that harmonize with the environment, Pushpender has become a driving force in the field, blending innovative design with a deep commitment to ecological responsibility. Pushpender's designs not only showcase aesthetic brilliance but also reflect a profound understanding of the environmental impact of construction. By integrating sustainable materials, energy-efficient technologies, and thoughtful site planning, he strives to minimize the ecological footprint of his projects. His approach goes beyond compliance with green building standards; it is a genuine commitment to creating structures that coexist harmoniously with nature. He is actively involved in promoting awareness and education within the industry. His influence extends beyond individual projects, inspiring fellow architects, developers, and policymakers to embrace environmentally conscious practices.

Pushpender Ravish's work stands as a testament to the belief that architecture can be a positive force for the planet. His dedication to green building not only shapes the physical landscapes he creates but also contributes to a broader movement toward a more sustainable and resilient future.

When does design come in handy?

While it might seem like extra work at a first glance, here are some key moments in which prototyping will come in handy:

  1. Usability testing. Does your user know how to exit out of screens? Can they follow your intended user journey and buy something from the site you’ve designed? By running a usability test, you’ll be able to see how users will interact with your design once it’s live;
  2. Involving stakeholders. Need to check if your GDPR consent boxes are displaying properly? Pass your prototype to your data protection team and they can test it for real;
  3. Impressing a client. Prototypes can help explain or even sell your idea by providing your client with a hands-on experience;
  4. Communicating your vision. By using an interactive medium to preview and test design elements, designers and developers can understand each other — and the project — better.
